The autism vocabulary

A Minanu (Noun)

Plural: Minanus

A Minanu is something that is very cute, adorable and usually very little. You can easily recognize a Minanu, by your own natural reaction. When coming across a Minanu, you will probably tend to say "mnmnmnmnnmnmn". Another (less yet still quite common) reaction is "Awhshshshshsh".

Examples of a Minanu: A kitty, a doggy, a small mouse (commonly small animals)

Use in sentence: "Ali come look at this kitty!" Ali: "Mnmnmnmnmnmnm. That one must be A Minanu!"

To be a Minanu (adjective)

Gradation: Minanu, bigger Minanu, the Minanuest

To be cute, adorable, lovable. Used to describe something small, cute, sweet.

Use in sentence: "The kitten was minanu. She was very sweet."

A Wiwiwi (Noun)

Plural: Wiwiwis

A Wiwiwi is very similar to a Minanu. Wiwiwis tend to be bigger in size, but still very adorable. Another difference is in the natural reaction. When seeing a Wiwiwi, you might feel the need to say "Wiwiwiiweiiwiwi". "Awhshshsh" is a reaction common for both, wiwiwis and minanus.

However a Wiwiwi and a Minanu can be used for any cute-looking creature. The choice depends on many other factors and both are suitable for most situations and creatures.

Examples of a Minanu: A cat, a dog, a horse (commonly bigger animals)

Use in sentence: "Ali, there's a big kitty waiting outside." Ali: "Wiwiwiwiwi! I will let that Wiwiwi inside."

To be a Wiwiwi (adjective)

Gradation: Wiwiwi, wiwier, the wiwiest

To be adorable, sweet, cute. Used for bigger creatures. Used to describe something cute and big.

Use in sentence: "This cat is so wiwiwiwi. She is very cute."

An Evil Weevil (Noun)

Plural: Evil Weevils

Evil Weevil is originally a snake-like creature, living in the Evil river. However, an Evil weevil can be any creature, that shows signs of bad intentions/has done harmful or generally considered bad things.

Examples of an Evil Weevil: kitty preparing to bite it's owner. A creature prepring to harm some other creature. An evil-looking creature.

Use in sentence: "No kitty, don't eat the mouse. You are an Evil Weevil!"

"The mouse looked like an Evil Weevil. She didn't want to be pet."

Can be used as an adjective but it is not very common. (Gradation: Evil Weevl, Eviler Weevil, the Evilest Weevl)

To be Evilsauce (adjective)

Gradation: Evilsauce, more evilsauce, the most evilsauce

Evilsauce is the negative version of sauce (check the meaning of "Sauce" on the next page). It describes something bad, evil or not good. Usually used for people or other living beings. Has similiar meaning as "Evil Weevil" (see previous page). Could be cnsidered as an adjective version of Evil Weevil.

Use in sentence: "The kitty is evilsauce. She almost bit me."

To be Sauce (adjective)

Gradation: Sauce, saucer, the saucest

The word "sauce" describes something that is good, fine or nice. It can describe a feeling, event, person or other nouns.

Use in sentence: "How are you doing today Ali?" Ali:"I'm doing sauce. The weather is nice."

"How was the event?""it was sauce. I enjoyed it".

"Who is that person?" "He is my friend. He is so sauce."

To be Blossom (adjective)

Gradation: Blossom, more blossom, the blossomest

Blossom is someone or something that's better than sauce, but not "awesomesauce" (see next - To be awesomesauce). Describes something good or amazing.

Use in sentence: "How are you doing today Ali?" "I'm doing blossom. I bought the snack I like."

"How was the event?" "It was blossom. I liked it a lot."

"Who is that person?" "He's my friend. He is so blossom, because he's very funny."

To be Awesomesauce (adjective)

Gradation: Awesomesauce, more awesomesauce, the most awesomesauce

Awesomesauce is quite similar to the word sauce and blossom. Can be used when talking about very good, amazing or beautiful things.

Use in sentence: "How are you doing today Ali?" "I'm awesomesauce! I just passed my exam!"

"How was the event?" "So awesomesauce. I loved it"

"Who is that person?" "That's Ali. He is so awesomesauce because he is very kind."

To be Awesome Blossom (adjective)

Gradation: Awesome blossom, more awesome blossom, the most awesome blossom

Awesome blossom takes the 2nd rank on the awesomeness scale. It describes something very exciting, beautiful or generally just completely awesome.

Use in sentence: "How are you doing today Ali?" "Awesome blossom! I found my lost cat today!"

"How was the event?" "It was awesome blossom. I will never forget it."

"Who is that person?" "He is awesome blossom. He gave me $200 just because we are friends."

To be Awesome Blossom Sauce (adjective)

Gradation: Awesome blossom sauce, more awesome blossom sauce, the most awesome blossom sauce

This adjective takes the 1st place on the awesomeness scale. It describes something completely amazing and awesome, flawless and perfect in all ways. Can be used to describe absolute astonishment

Use in sentence: "How are you doing today Ali?" "I'm doing awesome blossom sauce! I won 4 times in a row in league of legends (ranked)!"

"How was the event?" "It was awesome blossom sauce! I had the best time of my life!"

"Who is that person?" "That's Ali. He is so awesome blossom sauce. He's an amazing person!"

The scale of Awesomness

1. Awesome Blossom Sauce

2. Awesome Blossom

3. Awesomesauce

4. Blossom

5. Sauce

6. Evilsauce

7. Evil Weevil

A Sigma (Noun)

Plural: Sigmas

A Sigma is someone very cool or good. Usually used to describe people or other living beings. Sigmas are those, who act differently than others, in another words "they don't go with the flaw, but they act how they want to".

Examples of a Sigma: A person with sevreal signs of autism. A 12yo kid.

Use in sentence: "No, I am not going outside! I'm a Sigma!"

The word sigma can have also signs of an adjective ("I'm so sigma!").

To be Skibidi (adjective)

Gradation: Skibidi, skibidier, the skibidiest

To be skibidi, menas to be silly, nice or good. Generally a good meaning, with signs of autism. There isn't perfect definition of skibidi, but it coul be interpreted as "sauce"(sauce - see previous pages)

Use in sentence: "How are you doing today Ali?" "I'm skibidi. I have several autism."

Can be seen also as a verb ("Skibidiing around" - silly behaviour).

To eep (verb)

Tenses: past - ept, present cont. - eepying/eeping

To eep mena sto sleep but like a little cute kitty. There isn't much difference.

Use in sentence: "I just ept 14 hours!"

"Is Ali eepying already?" "Yes, he's like a kitty"

Adjective form: eepy (eepy, eepier, the eepiest)

Use in sentence: "He's the eepiest kitty in the town."

Noun form: eep

Use in sentence: "The eep is getting me."
